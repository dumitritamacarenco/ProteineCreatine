Project Mission

To develop a validated prototype for measuring physiological protein and creatine levels, bridging the gap between generic health guidelines and individual biological needs.

Executive Summary

Current health authorities provide static guidelines for protein intake (e.g., 0.8g/kg) that fail to account for the dynamic needs of aging populations, active individuals, and sex-specific variances. Furthermore, Creatine is often viewed solely as a sports supplement, despite robust clinical evidence supporting its role in cognitive preservation and sarcopenia prevention.

This project aims to disrupt the market by creating a feedback loop: Measure → Analyze → Optimize. By combining minimally invasive biosensing with algorithmic recommendations, we can offer the first "fuel gauge" for the body's structural and energetic building blocks.

Clinical Background: The Blind Spot

Protein: The Longevity Lever

While general guidelines suggest ~0.8g/kg/day, research shows this is insufficient for optimal aging. Anabolic resistance in older adults means they require significantly more protein to trigger muscle protein synthesis (MPS). The gap between "survival" intake and "optimal" intake is where longevity is determined.

Creatine: Beyond Muscle

Creatine is essential for the phosphocreatine energy system (ATP recycling). Beyond muscle, the brain utilizes ~20% of the body's energy. Low creatine levels correlate with fatigue and cognitive decline. Vegetarians and the elderly often present with sub-clinical deficiencies.

Key Research Insight

"The RDA for protein (0.8 g/kg) is a minimum to avoid deficiency, not an optimum for health. Active individuals and the elderly may need 1.2–2.0 g/kg." — Phillips et al., Frontiers in Nutrition.

The "Blue Ocean" Opportunity

The current market is polarized. On one end, we have General Health Apps (MyFitnessPal) which rely on manual input and generic databases. On the other, we have Specific Medical Devices (Abbott Lingo, Dexcom) focused heavily on Glucose and Ketones.

The Gap: There is no consumer-grade device for Anabolic Health (Protein/Creatine).

Key Competitors/Proxies:

  • Abbott Lingo: Consumer biowearable for glucose/ketones. High tech barrier.
  • Vivoo: Urine strip analysis via App. Good form factor, but low resolution data.
  • InsideTracker: Blood panel analysis. Accurate but episodic (not real-time).

Recommended Semester Roadmap

Given the diverse team (Food Science, Bioinfo, Business, Software), the project should run parallel workstreams: Technical Prototyping and Commercial Validation.

Phase 1: Discovery & Definition (Weeks 1-4)

Tech: Research enzymatic sensors for Creatine (Creatinase/Sarcosine Oxidase). Select "proxy" metrics for MVP app.
Business: Interview athletes & elderly (Sarcopenia risk). Map "Customer Journey" for testing.

Phase 2: Prototyping (Weeks 5-10)

Tech: Build iOS/Web App mockup connected to HealthKit. Attempt "Lab-on-chip" simulation or wet-lab proof of enzyme reaction.
Business: Regulatory deep dive (Medical vs Consumer device pathway). Cost analysis of sensor manufacturing.

Phase 3: Integration & Pitch (Weeks 11-14)

Tech: Demo Algorithm (Input data -> Protein Rec). Visual concept of hardware.
Business: Final Pitch Deck. Go-to-market strategy (B2C subscription model).
